Sunday 5th December 1920

reported in the Ledbury Guardian
Saturday 11th Dec 1920

LEST WE FORGET
Ledbury Honours its Dead Heroes
WAR MEMORIAL UNVEILED

About one hundred ex-Servicemen mar- shalled by Captain R.B.Gurney took part and at their head marched three represen-
tatives of the sister services in uniform. They were Company Quartermast-Ser geant F.Shinn, 1st Herefordshire Regiment, Corporal S.Smith R.A.F. and Stoker W. Neale as representing the Army, Air Force, and Navy respectively. A beautiful wreath was carried  being the ex-Servicemen's tri- bute to their dead comrades, The ceremony commenced  with the singing of the hymn "O God our help in ages past", following which the Rector of Ledbury (the Rev. F. W. Carnegy) offered prayer, during which a solemn silence prevailed.

  The memorial, which was covered by a large Union Jack, was then unveiled by General Sir Elliott Wood K.C.E.

[After the dedication by the Rev. Canon Bannister]

  The vast congregation sang Mr. J. S. Ark- wright's hymn-poem, "The Supreme Sacri- fice", set to music by the Rev. Dr. Charles Harris,  D.D.,  Rector of Colwall.  The beauty and sincerity of its language and sentiment, have endeared this hymn to countless congregations.
